      4.The two DJs, one based in Perth, and the other in San Francisco, remixed the Green
      Day album American Idiot with music from a variety of artists including Aerosmith
      and Eminem. When they posted their American Edit album into cyberspace for free, it
      became an instant hit and a copyright nightmare. It took just twelve days for the DJ
      team to receive a 'cease and desist' notice from Green Day's record label.
      The notice sparked outrage. More than 400,000 people signed an online petition
      protesting against the record label's moves to stop the album, which was only available
      on the Internet. Green Day came out publicly to say they were flattered by the album,
      and liked it.
      "We now inhabit a 'remix culture', a culture which is dominated by amateur creators –
      creators who are no longer willing to be passive recipients of content," recently wrote
      Australian lawyers from the Queensland University of Technology in their report
      Mashups, Remixes and Copyright Law.
      "Instead, they are demanding a much broader right, a right to mashup and remix
      material – to take on the role of producers – to cut, paste, sample of jam with content,
      in order to produce something which is distinctive of their own social and creative
      innovation."

      Questions to be answered:
      4.1. Should people be able to access copyright-protected material in order to create
      something else?
      No, I don't think people should be able to access copyright-protected material as this is a copyright infringement. They will need to ask permission from the owner of the material before using it and definitely before posting it online.
      Copyright infringement is an offence and damages the owner financially and harms
      society

      4.2. If they should, what rights do the original creators of the work have?
      The original creators have the right to own and control the work
      Rights to who can use the work and under what circumstances.
      They have the exclusive rights to copy, display perform, distribute and alter.

         Answers to tutorial:

        1. You have probably seen a lot of ‘grafitti’ on the walls near train stations, alleys and various places. Should photos of graffiti be licensed, if at all?

        When Graffiti is on public property and is essentially illegal, i.e. it is created on a property without the permission of the property owner, then no it cannot be licensed. However, if it is legal graffiti that is on a permitted property then it can be licensed.

        1. Your cousin owns a deli. He asks you to write an inventory program for him. You agreed to help him and do not charge for the program. The program works pretty well, and you discover later that your cousin has given copies to several friends who operate similar or smaller deli shops.
        2.1 Do you believe that your cousin should have asked for your permission?
        Yes the cousin should have asked for permission. Since I am the creator of the program, it is protected by copyright law.
        2.2 Do you believe that other merchants should pay you for the copies?

        Yes, they should be paying for using the software.

        1. Does Australian copyright law restrict one from lending/renting their computer game? What if your friend intends to copy the game?

        Lending a physical copy of a computer game on a CD is not restricted. But lending a copy electronically means that a copy would need to made, which will infringe the copyright. 

        1. Read the scenario and answer the questions that follow (pay attention, this is a typical examination questions).

        Questions to be answered:

        4.1 Should people be able to access copyright-protected material in order to create something else?

        No, with copyright-protected material, people do not have access to it to create something else. The creator is the one who controls how the material is used and for what purposes. 
        4.2 If they should, what rights do the original creators of the work have?
        The original creator needs to be asked for permission, for their copyright-protected work to be used. 
        1. What is meant by the term Cloud Computing? Discuss some known legal issues that surround the use of this 
        Cloud Computing is the term used where hardware and software are used to deliver a service over a network. This is done over the internet. 
        Since cloud computing requires users to store data and content that is copyright-protected, there are concerns of copyright infringement.

            Games and Copyright (G016v10) FINAL.pdf (Links to an external site.)
            Lending or renting games
            Copyright law does not restrict people from lending items. However, owners of copyright in computer software and sound recordings have the right to control rental of these items. If money changes hands in connection with lending an item which includes computer software or a sound recording, the transaction may infringe copyright unless the copyright owner has given permission. For further information, see our information sheets Renting Items Protected by Copyright and Lending Items Protected by Copyright.
            Note that if you lend a game to someone knowing that they intend to copy it, you might both be liable for copyright infringement, since it is an infringement to authorise another person to infringe copyright. For further information, see our information sheet Infringement - Actions, Remedies, Offences and Penalties.
